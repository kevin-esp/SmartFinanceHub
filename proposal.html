Smart Finance Hub

Helping Young Adults Make Smarter Financial Decisions

Website Project Proposal

Site Title

Smart Finance Hub

Developer

Kevin Espinoza

Rationale / Focus

Smart Finance Hub is an educational website designed to help young adults between the ages of 18 and 25 build a strong understanding of personal finance. Rather than simply telling users what financial decisions they should make, the website teaches them how to evaluate financial choices by understanding costs, risks, benefits, and long-term consequences.

The website combines educational articles with interactive financial calculators to explain budgeting, saving, investing, credit, loans, amortization, APR, opportunity cost, hidden ownership costs, and other important financial concepts. The goal is to help users become more confident when making real-world financial decisions.

Main Elements Outline

  • Home: Introduction to the website, featured articles, and quick links to financial tools.
  • Budgeting & Saving: Budget planning, emergency funds, saving strategies, and financial goal setting.
  • Credit & Debt: Credit scores, credit reports, responsible credit card use, good debt versus bad debt, and debt management.
  • Loans & Financing: APR, interest rates, loan terms, amortization, auto loans, student loans, mortgages, and responsible borrowing.
  • Investing: Stocks, ETFs, retirement accounts, compound interest, diversification, and long-term investing.
  • Financial Decision Making: Opportunity cost, hidden ownership costs, depreciation, total cost of ownership, paying cash versus financing, leveraging debt responsibly, and evaluating financial tradeoffs.
  • Financial Calculators: APR Calculator, Loan Payment Calculator, Amortization Calculator, Compound Interest Calculator, Savings Goal Calculator, Budget Calculator, and Total Cost of Ownership Calculator.
  • Resources: Financial glossary, frequently asked questions, recommended resources, and educational articles.
  • Contact: Contact information and user feedback form.

Estimated Content

The completed website is expected to contain approximately 20 to 30 individual web pages. These pages will include educational articles, financial calculators, resource pages, and supporting content designed to help visitors better understand personal finance.

Target Audience

Smart Finance Hub is designed primarily for young adults between the ages of 18 and 25. This includes high school graduates, college students, recent graduates, first-time employees, and anyone beginning to manage their own finances.

The website is intended for users with little or no financial experience. Content will be written using clear language, practical examples, and interactive tools that make complex financial topics easier to understand.

Design Considerations

  • Simple and intuitive navigation.
  • Professional and modern appearance.
  • Responsive layout for desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
  • Accessible design using semantic HTML and WCAG accessibility principles.
  • Consistent color palette and typography throughout the website.
  • Interactive financial calculators for improved user engagement.
  • Easy-to-read educational content with practical examples.

Limiting Factors

The website must remain understandable for users with limited financial knowledge, requiring financial concepts to be explained in simple language. Development time and the scope of the course project may limit the number of interactive features included. The website should remain compatible with modern web browsers and responsive across multiple screen sizes. All information is intended for educational purposes and should not be considered professional financial advice.
